Battle Bots

Competitive Combat Robot Engineered for Performance and Durability

Mechanical Design Electronics Control Systems 3D Printing

Project Overview

Designed and fabricated a combat robot for a collegiate BattleBots competition, prioritizing chassis durability and drivetrain reliability over offensive weaponry. The goal was to maximize survivability under high-energy impacts while maintaining full mobility throughout matches.

Design Approach

The chassis was plasma-cut from aluminum plate with reinforced gussets and strategic ribbing to resist impact loads at motor mounts and corner junctions. A custom 3D-printed belted gearbox was chosen over a rigid geartrain to dampen transient loads while delivering consistent torque, paired with a four-wheel drive layout for traction and maneuverability.

Manufacturing

Chassis plates were plasma-cut for accuracy, with drivetrain housings 3D printed for rapid iteration. Assembly combined bolted fasteners for serviceability with welded joints at high-stress interfaces, producing a lightweight but impact-resistant structure within the competition weight limit.

Testing and Validation

FEA in SolidWorks validated the chassis under simulated impact conditions using Von Mises stress plots and safety factor mapping, with buckling and fatigue checks on critical frame members. Physical drop-weight and staged impact tests confirmed the chassis absorbed forces without yielding, while drivetrain trials verified torque output and thermal limits under extended runtime.

Results

In competition, the robot consistently outperformed expectations, sustaining repeated direct strikes without structural failure while maintaining full mobility throughout matches. The reinforced chassis successfully absorbed and redistributed impact forces, preventing bending or cracking in critical load-bearing members. The drivetrain proved exceptionally resilient, delivering reliable torque and maneuverability even under repeated collisions. While the absence of an active weapon limited offensive capability, the design’s defensive robustness allowed it to outlast more aggressive opponents whose systems failed under combat stress. Ultimately, the robot secured 1st place out of 10 competing teams, validating the effectiveness of a durability-first design approach and demonstrating the value of robust chassis reinforcement and drivetrain reliability in combat robotics.
