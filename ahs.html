Project Overview

Designed and built an automated hydroponics system to provide reliable year-round plant growth in underserved communities in West Lafayette, Indiana. The goal was a low-cost, weather-resistant system that could run with minimal human input while applying professional engineering practices in CAD modeling, automation, and validation.

Design

All subsystems were modeled in Fusion 360. Grow modules were 3D-printed with optimized channel geometry for uniform nutrient flow, with parametric net cup holders for flexibility across plant types. Fluid circulation used a 12V submersible pump with a gravity-fed return line, controlled by an Arduino monitoring pH and temperature with automatic shutoffs when conditions drifted outside safe ranges.

Constraints

Deployed outdoors in Indiana, weather resistance drove material choices — PETG and ABS for printed parts, food-grade HDPE for the reservoir, and IP67-rated connectors for electronics. Cost and replicability were equally critical, so all components were sourced from widely available off-the-shelf parts to keep the system accessible and manufacturable without specialized tooling.

Validation

Flow distribution and structural loads were validated in Fusion 360 before physical testing. Leak checks, pressure measurements, and endurance runs confirmed flow rates held within ±5% across all grow sites, and the pump completed over 500 duty cycles without failure.

Technical Specifications

Mechanical / Structural
  • Frame: 20×20 mm aluminum extrusion with acrylic panels
  • Grow modules: 3D-printed PETG/ABS, 2 mm wall thickness, 0.2 mm layer height
  • Net cups: 2 in (50 mm) diameter, parametric design for alternative sizes
  • Reservoir capacity: 15 L food-grade HDPE tank
  • System footprint: 600 × 400 × 500 mm
Fluid Delivery
  • Pump: 12 V DC submersible, max flow 3 L/min, operating at ~1.5 L/min
  • Max pump head: 0.5 m
  • Flow variation across grow sites: ±5%
  • Duty cycle: 15 minutes on / 45 minutes off
  • Mesh filtration: 200 µm stainless steel filter at manifold inlet
Sensors and Controls
  • Microcontroller: Arduino Uno
  • pH probe: range 0–14, accuracy ±0.1
  • Temperature probe: DS18B20, accuracy ±0.5 °C
  • Water level sensor: capacitive, range 0–200 mm
  • RTC module: DS3231, battery-backed for power resilience
  • Indicators: tri-color LED status display and buzzer alerts
Electrical / Power
  • Power input: 12 V DC, 2 A max (24 W)
  • Control circuit: MOSFET-based switching, with 5 V logic rail
  • Connectors: IP67-rated waterproof plugs
  • Safety: inline 2 A fuse, waterproof housing for electronics
Environmental Performance
  • Operating temperature: 5–35 °C
  • Reservoir insulation: maintains nutrient solution 18–24 °C in ambient range 10–30 °C
  • Outdoor resistance: PETG/ABS parts UV-stable and weather resistant
  • Maximum crop sites: 6 plants per module, expandable with additional manifolds
Output / Validation
  • Typical growth cycle: 3–4 weeks for leafy greens (lettuce, basil, spinach)
  • Plant survival rate: >75% during trial runs
  • Assembly time: <30 minutes with only basic tools
  • System endurance: >500 pump duty cycles validated without failure
