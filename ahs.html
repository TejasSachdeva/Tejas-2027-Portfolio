Low-Cost Automated Hydroponics System for Community Deployment

Project Overview

Designed and deployed an automated hydroponics system to provide reliable year-round plant growth in underserved communities in West Lafayette, Indiana. The system was prototyped in Fusion 360 and initially 3D printed for testing, then transitioned to off-the-shelf components to make it replicable without access to specialized tools or equipment.

Design

The system was modeled in Fusion 360 to validate layout, fluid routing, and component fit before physical assembly. Final construction used a wire shelving rack for the frame, PVC channels for grow sites, standard tubing and fittings for fluid delivery, and a plastic storage bin as the reservoir. An Arduino monitored pH and temperature and controlled pump cycling, with automatic shutoffs when conditions drifted outside safe ranges.

Constraints

Deployed outdoors in Indiana, weather resistance and cost drove every decision. All components were chosen from widely available off-the-shelf parts so the system could be replicated by community members without access to 3D printers or fabrication tools. Electronics were enclosed in waterproof housings with IP67-rated connectors for outdoor reliability.

Validation

Flow distribution and structural layout were validated in Fusion 360 before assembly. Physical testing confirmed flow rates held within ±5% across all grow sites, and the pump completed over 500 duty cycles without failure. Sensors were calibrated against lab standards for pH and temperature.

Technical Specifications

Mechanical / Structural
  • Frame: Wire shelving rack
  • Grow channels: PVC pipe
  • Reservoir: Plastic storage bin
Fluid Delivery
  • Pump: 12 V DC submersible, ~1.5 L/min
  • Flow variation: ±5% across grow sites
  • Duty cycle: 15 minutes on / 45 minutes off
Controls
  • Microcontroller: Arduino Uno
  • Sensors: pH (±0.1), temperature (±0.5°C), water level
  • Auto shutoff when conditions drift out of range
Electrical
  • Power: 12V DC, 24W max
  • Connectors: IP67-rated waterproof
Performance
  • Operating temperature: 5–35 °C
  • Plant survival rate: >75%
  • Assembly time: <30 minutes
  • Pump endurance: >500 cycles validated
