Project Overview

Designed and built a custom RC car from scratch, optimizing the drivetrain, steering geometry, and structural layout for competitive racing. The project spanned mechanical design, DFM, FEA validation, and electronics integration.

Drivetrain Design

The initial gear drivetrain used a two-stage spur arrangement for a 16:1 reduction, but produced gear whine, backlash, and tooth chipping under load. We redesigned around an HTD synchronous belt system for a ~10.5:1 reduction — quieter, smoother, and significantly more reliable. Stress concentrations were reduced and vibration-induced wear was essentially eliminated, with only a minor efficiency trade-off from 97% to 94%.

Since all structural components were 3D printed, DFM was a key consideration. Given FDM's anisotropic nature, we added lead-in chamfers on mating features for ease of assembly and fastening, and ensured all sharp edges had generous fillets to reduce stress concentrations at layer interfaces.

Tolerancing

To ensure a reliable press fit on the hub adapter, we referenced standard H7 tolerance charts to define the allowable bore dimensions. This kept the fit consistent across printed parts without requiring custom measurement tooling.

Simulation & Steering

We ran a basic FEA on the hub adapter to identify peak stress regions and confirm the design wouldn't fail under expected loads. Ackermann steering geometry was implemented to minimize wheel scrub, with a measured turning radius of 347mm closely matching the CAD prediction of 320mm.
